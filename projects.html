projects.
LITMUS
september 2023 - current

i have always been fascinated by colour and its applications. the fact that certain pigments, certain interactions of light, can evoke certain emotions, is beautiful.

colour's applications in branding are no less beautiful. in an ever-expanding social world, communication is of utmost importance, and in branding, colour is often communication's foundation, and choosing the right colours for the right message is no mean feat.

this is what LITMUS assists with.

LITMUS is your guide to colour psychology. it allows users to pick up to three colours, and provides a visual guide of what those colours mean, and how they might complement each other. A litmus test, if you will.

wireframed using Figma, programmed with HTML/CSS, JavaScript.

SWIM
september 2023 - december 2023

and here is the product of that class's lessons. i love watching video games just as much as, if not more than, playing them. playthroughs are cinema to me. i've lost hours to video game playthroughs, immersing myself in their worlds, their characters, their stories. i'm a bookworm, and what bookworm doesn't love a good story, no matter the medium?

SWIM is the first video game i've ever created. it is a study of the video game medium, a chance for me to practice video game design and learn the importance of digital aesthetics. i created the graphics with Midjourney's generative AI feature, finetuned them with Adobe Photoshop, and programmed my game with the Unity game engine.

SWIM is a simple game, my first, but by no means my last. i cannot wait to explore game design further with all the skills i learned during this project's creation. cannot wait to tell my own stories this way.

graphics created with Midjourney and Adobe Photoshop, programmed with Unity.

AuraL
march 2023 - may 2023

AuraL is my most recent foray into mobile app programming. its title is a blend of the words aura (relating to mood) and aural (relating to sound), and that is exactly the kind of experience it promises.

AuraL combines mood diary with music recommendations, allowing users to select from multiple music streaming services, and multiple moods, providing playlists for each service that align with those moods.

i often find myself switching between music streaming services AND moods on repeat, searching for a specific sound that may exist on one app, but not another. AuraL fixes that frustration, providing a hub for any streaming service you might need, and any mood you might feel.

programmed with Swift.
